Investment Thesis

We back teams building useful on-chain infrastructure.

We care more about whether the product solves a real problem, whether the mechanism holds up, and whether the team can keep shipping through long cycles.

Our attention stays on settlement architecture, capital efficiency, security boundaries, and the rails bringing institutional demand into on-chain markets.

We prefer a small number of positions where product direction, fundraising prep, and launch sequencing still benefit from direct work.

01

Start with product and mechanism

We look at demand and design before we look at storytelling.

02

Fewer bets, deeper work

We would rather spend time where sustained involvement is useful.

03

Concrete support

Token design, fundraising prep, ecosystem introductions, and launch readiness.

01 Crypto-native only. We mainly look for products where settlement, coordination, and incentives are part of the product itself.
02 Prefer the underlying rails. We are more interested in products other teams have to depend on than in short-lived wrapper layers.
03 Judge over time. We care more about whether a team can compound through cycles than whether the story looks good for one quarter.
